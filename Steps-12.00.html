KPI 1.)

SALES:  This KPI will methodically have screen shots and lots of explanation compared to the rest of the KPIs.  After getting past this one and learn how to build this one, the rest will be exactly the same setup but different color schemes, numbers and scripts. 

Step 1:  See the below GIF and screen shot.  On the sheet, click on "Assets", then click on "Custom Objects", and finally drag and drop "#1 Vizlib KPI" to the top left of the sheet.  For now do not worry about the size; in a few steps it will be custom sized. 



Step 2:  After dragging a KPI onto the sheet from step one, go all the way to the right where before adding the 3 "Layers" and all its components, add a border at the top of the KPI visual under the "APPERANCE" tab.  This step is needed for ALL four KPI charts.  

Process:  Click on "Appearance", then under this tab click on "Container Styling."  Under Container Styling, scroll down to "Border" and make sure it is green as with #3; make the "Border Style" solid; make the "Border Color" black (#000000); make the "Border Width" 30px; and make the "Border Radius" 0px. (See screen shot below for visual)    

Step 2: "LAYERS"

Within this first KPI notice the three corresponding layers that you will see in the next few KPI's as well.

NOTE:  The below screen shot shows where to find the three different layers for this KPI and once you create future KPIs, they will need to have layers added as well.


Layer 1 (Sales, title)-

Process:  Please see the GIF below for visual instructions as well.  After dragging and dropping a KPI chart onto the sheet, go all the way to the right of the sheet using the "Properties" panel click "Layers" then click "Add Layer."  After adding the first layer proceed to the "Layer Name" and add the name "Total Sales" (or any name you choose); click on [A] "Text;" scroll down to "Text" and add the KPI's title, "SALES;" scroll down to "Horizontal Alignment" which should be centered and "Vertical Alignment" which should be "Top;" scroll down to "Font Color" which should be white (#ffffff), "Font Style" is "Bold" and "Font Family" is "Times New Roman;" scroll down to "Auto Font Size" and make sure this is off so you can chose the size; the "Font Size" should be 20px; as you scroll down to "Position" make sure that "Background Color, Layer Border, and Enable Tooltip" are off.  Lastly, under "Position" make sure of the following: X-Pos [31.58] Width [33.55];  Y-Pos [-44.26] Height [32.79] (adjust these numbers as needed to fit).  This completes the first layer.    


Layer 2 (Sales #)-

Process:  Please see the GIF below for visual instructions as well.  Like the first layer, after dragging and going all the way to the right of the sheet using the "Properties" panel click "Layers" then click "Add Layer."  After adding a 2nd layer proceed to the "Layer Name" and add the name "Total Sales #" (or any name you choose); click on [A] "Text;" scroll down and click on "Format as Number" and a SCRIPT located below this paragraph needs to be added to the "Text" box after clicking on "fx;" go to "Num. Format" and choose "10k;" go to "Negative Format" and click "-10k;" next, click on and activate "Currency Format;" next, click "Prefix" under "Position;" next, make sure that "Indicators" is disabled; next, "Horizontal Alignment" should be centered and "Vertical Alignment" which should be "Middle;" scroll down to "Font Color" which should be blue (#192ba8), "Font Style" is "Bold" and "Font Family" is "Times New Roman;" scroll down to "Auto Font Size" and make sure this is off so you can chose the size; the "Font Size" should be 30px; as you scroll down to "Position" make sure that "Background Color, Layer Border, and Enable Tooltip" are off.  Lastly, under "Position" make sure of the following: X-Pos [8.29] Width [66.21];  Y-Pos [7.06] Height [38.82].  This completes the 2nd layer.    

SCRIPT:  Sum([Units Sold] * [Price per Unit])


Layer 3 (Sales YoY%)-

Process:  Please see the GIF below for visual instructions as well.  Like the first two layers, after dragging and going all the way to the right of the sheet using the "Properties" panel click "Layers" then click "Add Layer."  After adding a 3rd layer proceed to the "Layer Name" and add the name "# Sale YoY%%" (or any name you choose); click on [A] "Text;" scroll down and click on "Format as Number" and a SCRIPT located below this paragraph needs to be added to the "Text" box after clicking on "fx;" go to "Num. Format" and choose "100%;" go to "Negative Format" and click "-100%;" next, click on "Indicators" to make it "Enabled", then make the "Indicator Threshold," "0.51," then, make the "Fa-Arrow-Up" have the color green (#008000), then, make the "Fa-Arrow-Down" have the color red (#ff0000), then, scroll down and the "Space between the icon and text" should be at "3;" next, "Horizontal Alignment" should be left and "Vertical Alignment" should be "Top;" scroll down to "Font Color" which should be baby blue (#89cff0), "Font Style" is "Bold" and "Font Family" is "Times New Roman;" scroll down to "Auto Font Size" and make sure this is off so you can chose the size; the "Font Size" should be 18px; as you scroll down to "Position" make sure that "Background Color, Layer Border, and Enable Tooltip" are off.  Lastly, under "Position" make sure of the following: X-Pos [61.46] Width [37.44];  Y-Pos [9.41] Height [41.18].  This completes the 3rd layer and first KPI!    

SCRIPT:  SUM({<Size={'2','3','4','5','6','6.5','7','7.5','8', '8.5','9','9.5','10','10.5','11','11.5','12','12.5','13','13.5','14'}>}                                                                  "Price per Unit" * "Units Sold") /  SUM({<Size=>} "Price per Unit" * "Units Sold")
