The Map

How the final product map should look.  The map has a title, a key (for the point layer), and a layer showing a region layer.  The map's area layers are colored based on the different regions which include West, South, Southwest, Northeast, and Midwest.  If you take a quick glance at the map you can immediately see which states at the point layer and what regions at the area layer sell well.

The Basics

This map is under "Charts."  Locate "Map" then drag-and-drop the map onto the sheet.  After clicking on the map and adding layers, this map deals with the "Area Layer" and "Point Layer."  Lastly, make the map a "Master Item."

Layer Steps

Remember the Mapping section from the data load editor?  The layers in the map are pulling the data from this table through ApplyMap().  Use the ApplyMap() names like "GroupRegional" for steps 1 & 2 in the Area screen shot directions.

Point Layer:  There is only one Point Layer in the map.
A. Expression (#3-"Field" under "Sales by Region"):  =If("State" <> 'Alaska' and "State" <> 'Hawaii', State)
B. Expression (#5-"Size by" under "Size & Shape"):  =SUM({<Region=>} "Price per Unit" * "Units Sold")
